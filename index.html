    Cognitive Computing : Building your own Nano-Watson
    Hall A

    History was created when Watson won the Jeopardy contest overcoming two of the most successful human contestants ever. This sparked the era of commercialisation of Cognitive Computing capabilities - IBM leading the way with advanced research on Watson. With the explosion of big data, IoT devices, smart machines, Cognitive Computing is all poised to forever change the way humans interact with machines. This session aims to give a primer on Cognitive Computing, explore the architecture behind Watson, and some real time use cases related to our business context. We also intend to give a sneak peek into a Watson-inspired cognitive engine, which we call as nano-Watson.

  • Mobile Testing: An Evolution
    Hall B

    Constant evolution of the methods and approaches for Mobile Application Testing is needed. Key challenges manifest due to the increasing variety of Mobile Devices, differences in screen dimensions, input methods, different hardware capabilities, diversity in operating system platforms. Testing a single application across multiple devices running on the same platform and every platform poses a unique challenge for testers. Our Mobile Application testing strategy has evolved over time to handle the challenges. This session aims to review the derivation of ever changing Mobile Application Testing strategy.

    IBM Websphere Portal 8.5 - The Digital Experience Platform
    Hall A

    Portal 8.5 brings a whole new array of features to its cap. In this session we will explore DDC (Digital Data Connector) and WAB (Web Application Bridge). DDC is used to integrate data from external data sources on your portal pages by using IBM Web Content Manager presentation components. It can render social data that you have on your IBM Connections server or on other social platforms in the context of your portal pages. Other possible data sources include news feeds, task lists, product catalog information, to name just a few. The IBM Web Application Bridge (or WAB) is a feature that uses reverse proxy technology to integrate different web applications natively inside WebSphere Portal pages so that WebSphere Portal becomes the single window of access to users.

  • Hybris Unveiled : Ten things you must know!
    Hall B

    In a short span of 3 years between 2010 and 2013, Hybris has graduated from being a niche player to a leader in Gartner's magic quadrant for eCommerce vendors. With the recent acquisition by SAP and significant momentum with Tier 1 and Tier 2 players, Hybris in eCommerce platform is probably poised to do what Facebook did to MySpace and other social networks. We, at Avnet, have been investing significant efforts in getting ourselves enabled in this platform to ride this wave. In this context, this session will discuss our perspective on the top 10 reasons on how differentiates itself from other Tier 1 players. The discussion will be a blend of technical and business related findings and observations.

    QA tools 2014
    Hall A

    Innovation is the key to future success. We would like to showcase two such innovations.

    SamePage
    An interactive, anytime, anywhere power-packed analytics tool targeted at executive level audience to enable data driven decision making for SDLC projects - that are managed through various ALM tools.

    Compatibility Testing Matrix - Gen Engine
    Testing across browsers has always been a high effort job, because of the various rendering engines of browsers, versions, and supporting operating systems and the mobile versions of the browsers. To test every page/screen of every application (Web and mobile) on every version of browser and operating system combination available in the market is not feasible. The Compatibility Testing Matrix - Gen Engine is an evolving algorithm which can be utilized to help arrive at a feasible and optimal percentage of test coverage across different browsers.

    Enhancing QA Effectiveness: QA Best Practices
    Hall A

    The ever-increasing complexity of today's software and their target deployments, combined with greater competitive pressures have escalated the need for testing to new heights. The testing team work under perennial combination of circumstances such as compressed schedules, fluid requirements and delays in various upstream dependencies. Facing these challenges has brought to light new solutions and approaches. Many of the key solutions can and have been converted to best practices, to be applied to new/upcoming projects. We would like to walk you through the various interesting facets of the Quality Assurance practice.

  • XDX and Commerce Integration patterns
    Hall B

    While the pace of business and the speed of content continue to accelerate, so does the demand to reach your audiences through a rich and robust online experience, through multiple channels and devices. IBM's XDX is uniquely designed to help create, manage, simplify and integrate your processes to meet these demands. IBM WebSphere Commerce is a cross-channel e-commerce platform that enables high-volume business-to-consumer (B2C) and business-to-business (B2B) sales to customers using multiple channels-web, mobile, social, store or phone. It supports better marketing, selling and fulfilment with advanced web and mobile stores, social integration and personalised marketing and merchandising tools. In this session we will see how to integrate these XDX and commerce technologies together for a better customer experience.
